Section 1

Explain how technology, and computer science specifically, have altered the way people interact with information since the industrial revolution.

During the Industrial Revolution, Johannes Gutenberg invented the printing press. This invention allowed citizens to read stories and books (mostly religion and science based). Eventually, they started making newspapers that people could read to catch up on news in their town or country. For the first time, people were gaining current knowledge in ways that were not possible before, and they could use this to make informed decisions about their daily lives.

In the new age of technology we have hundreds of thousands of online news sources. We receive alerts for breaking news from major sources right on our phones. We can read news articles seconds after they are released.
